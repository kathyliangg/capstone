initial research

Problem Statement
Rates of fishing gear are increasing due to the increase of discarded waste from people fishing, leading to further environmental damage and increase the death rate for animals in the ocean.
Need Statement
As a result of this issue, the community needs to create better ways to discard fishing gear so it will not further harm the environment.

Most modern fishing gear is made of long-lasting and/or synthetic materials, such as plastic and metal, that can remain in the environment for many years
A NOAA-funded study estimated that there are 145,000 derelict crab pots in the Chesapeake Bay, which can continue to kill 3.3 million blue crabs every year.

Recycling - One way to prevent derelict gear from becoming “ghost gear” is to have fishermen return their worn-out nets and traps to their port for recycling instead of dumping it into the ocean.
Clean-ups - Another solution is for organizations to pick up derelict fishing gear from the water.
Biodegradable fishing nets - A new study published in the journal Animal Conservation describes some promising tests done with biodegradable fishing nets. The researchers developed a net made of a blend of 82 percent polybutylene succinate (PBS) and 18 percent polybutylene adipate-co-terephthalate (PBAT) and compared its fishing efficiency with conventional nets.
